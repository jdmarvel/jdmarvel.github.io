ABOUT

I research how citizens engage with government performance and how statistical methods can illuminate public sector challenges.

My work sits at the intersection of quantitative methodology, public administration, and democratic theory. I use experimental designs, large-scale data analysis, and computational approaches to understand citizen attitudes toward federal agencies and policy effectiveness.

Currently investigating how AI and large language models influence democratic discourse and public opinion formation about government institutions.
