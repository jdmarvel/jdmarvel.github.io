About

I'm an Associate Professor of Public Administration at American University in Washington, DC, where I teach quantitative methods and research design to MPA and Ph.D. students.

My research examines how citizens form judgments about government performance, with a focus on experimental methods and statistical analysis. I'm particularly interested in the role of artificial intelligence in shaping democratic discourse and public opinion.
